## **Zhangye: A Hidden Gem on the Silk Road**

---

### **1. Introduction & Location**

Hello everyone!
Today, I’d like to introduce you to **Zhangye**, a fascinating city located in **Gansu Province**, in northwestern China.

Zhangye lies in the **middle of the Hexi Corridor**, a key passage of the ancient **Silk Road**.It takes up the entire breadth of the province, running from Inner Mongolia on the north to Qinghai on the south, but its urban core is at Ganzhou in the oasis formed by the Hei River. This city is known for its **unique natural landscapes**,and **rich cultural history**.

---

### **2. Culture & History**

- Zhangye has a deep historical background, especially during the **Han Dynasty**
  - It was one of the **four key cities** in the **Hexi Corridor**, along with **Wuwei**, **Jiuquan**, and **Dunhuang**. These cities were crucial military and trade outposts that helped protect and expand China’s influence into Central Asia.
    - The name **“Zhangye”** (张掖) has a very symbolic meaning. It comes from the phrase:
    > **“张国臂掖，以通西域”**
    > which means **“Extend the arm of the nation to reach the Western Regions.”**
    - This name reflects the strategic importance of Zhangye as a gateway between China and the western regions, including Central Asia and beyond.

The famous explorer **Marco Polo** is believed to have passed through Zhangye and mentioned it in his travel writings.

---

### **3. Tourist Attractions**

Zhangye offers a wide variety of tourist attractions, ranging from breathtaking natural landscapes to fascinating cultural sites.

- **Zhangye National Geopark**
 Known as the **“Rainbow Mountains”**, Zhangye Danxia is one of China’s most stunning landscapes, famous for its **vivid colors and striking shapes**.
 Formed over **24 million years**, layers of **sandstone and minerals** were uplifted by tectonic forces and then **shaped by wind and rain** into colorful **towers, cliffs, and ravines**.
 The scenery changes with the light, making it a dream spot for photographers.
- **Great Buddha Temple (Dafo Temple)**
 The **Dafo Temple** houses a **35-meter-long reclining Buddha**, built around **1100** during the **Western Xia**. Made of **clay on a wooden frame**, it’s the **largest reclining clay Buddha in China** and has remained mostly unchanged for nearly a thousand years.The Buddha rests in a grand hall, one of the few remaining wooden structures from the Qing dynasty.A mural inside vividly depicts Xuanzang’s journey to the West, featuring scenes of him on horseback.

- **Zhangye Bell and Drum Tower**
    The **Zhangye Bell and Drum Tower** is a **three-story wooden structure** built in the shape of a traditional Chinese tower, located in the center of city. It features **upturned eaves**, **elaborately carved beams**, and a **refined yet majestic architectural design**.
 The tower houses many **inscribed plaques**, which reflect Zhangye’s **historical importance** in terms of **geography, politics, military strategy**, and **culture**.
 As a **landmark building** of Zhangye, the Bell and Drum Tower stands as a silent witness to the city’s long and changing history through the ages.

### **4. Local Cuisine**

Zhangye’s food is simple, hearty, and full of local flavor.

- **Beef Xiaofan** (牛肉小饭) is a unique local dish from Zhangye. Although the name means “small rice,” it’s actually made from tiny pieces of **hand-cut noodles**, boiled until tender.
 The dish is served with **beef slices, tofu, and chewy starch noodles**, all in a flavorful **beef broth**.

- **Ganzhou Saozi Noodles** (甘州臊面) is a well-known traditional dish , especially popular as a **local breakfast**.
 The noodles are praised for being **thin, clear, and chewy**, and are hand-rolled into long, ribbon-like strips.
 The soup, often made with **chicken broth**, is seasoned with **pepper, ginger, vinegar**, and soy sauce, then topped with **diced pork, fried tofu, scallions**, and **cilantro** for a rich, savory flavor.

### **6. Conclusion**

To sum up, **Zhangye** is a perfect combination of **natural beauty**, **historical depth**, and **local charm**.
It may not be as famous as Beijing or Chongqing, but it offers something truly unique and unforgettable.
If you ever plan a trip to China’s west, don’t miss the chance to explore this hidden gem on the Silk Road!
**In fact, my grandmother is from Zhangye, and she often makes traditional Zhangye dishes at home.**
Thanks to her, I grew up tasting the authentic flavors of the region — and that’s part of why I feel such a strong connection to this amazing city.
